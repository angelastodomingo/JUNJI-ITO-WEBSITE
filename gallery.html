Azami's Scar - Ito's most iconic drawing
From: Uzumaki
This is one of Junji Ito's famous manga panels (if not all time!). You have probably seen this yourself, floating around social media. This is Azami, a character from a chapter on Uzumaki, who has been unfortunately cursed with the spiral.
Yasumin gets smashed!
From: Smashed
The depiction of his horrible death is really frightening. Readers may tell it's him because his silhouette and glasses are clearly visible amidst the blood. It's the first time the characters completely understand what's going on, and the first fatality among them perfectly foreshadows the horror to follow.
Full Body Tattoo
From: Lovesickness
The plot revolves around a boy named Ryusuke who meets a woman with progressively increasing difficulties over the course of several nights. After saying that she wants to acquire a tattoo one night, she emerges completely covered with them. Ito's design for this appearance exudes an unsettling and obsessive feel.
Shuichi's Father Becomes the Spiral!
From: Uzumaki
The Spiral Obsession follows Shuichi's father as he becomes obsessed with spirals. As the story progresses, his fascination becomes increasingly frightening, as he effortlessly curls his tongue and spins his eyes in spirals. His death has become one of Ito's most horrific pictures, as he is discovered in a big tub with his body distorted into a spiral.
Chibaru's Deflating Head
From: Hanging Balloons
When the balloon, which represents a girl named Chiharu, is shot and deflates, Ito demonstrates that whatever happens to the balloon will also happen to the person. The way Chiharu's skull deflates is both surprising and distressing because it is depicted in such detail, emphasizing the girl's death.
Mr. Yokota becomes a snail
From: Uzumaki
He's missing his legs and has a shell, yet his hands pull him into the classroom, his head fully intact. However, his eyes have been replaced by stalks, and his mouth is gradually disappearing as ridged pimples cover his flesh.
The Centipede
From: Honored Ancestors
This artwork from Honored Ancestors displays a parent connecting to his ancestors' skulls. There's something strange and fascinating about the design of this creature. The way the skulls are assembled to create a centipede-like appearance makes it difficult to look away, no matter how frightening it is.
The Rib Woman
From: Lovesickness
In the tale from Ito's Lovesickness collection, a woman who had rib surgery done to slim down experiences a strange noise all night long. As it happens, this is the creation of the Rib Woman, a persona that seems amiable and who is using a rib bone to perform music.
A painting of Tomie
From: Painter
In Painter's story, Tomie becomes the inspiration for a budding artist who is compelled to capture her beauty on canvas. His last masterpiece depicts Tomie in all her splendor with an incredible second head growing out of her, after multiple failed attempts that she laughed off. This is one of the most well-known depictions of Tomie.
Collon's treasure
From: Souichi's Beloved Pet
This picture depicts the family cat that Ito's character notoriously evil Souichi cursed, bringing up a terrifying scene. It's "one of those insects that live in Hell," as Souichi puts it, and Ito's portrayal of it is too intricate for the human mind to process.
